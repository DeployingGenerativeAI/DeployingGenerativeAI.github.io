Learning from Medical Time Series

Time series data are ubiquitous in healthcare and present an exciting opportunity for machine learning methods to extract actionable insights about human health. However, gaps remain to make machine learning systems practical and deployable for healthcare. This is because learning from time series is notoriously challenging: labels are often noisy or missing, data are extremely high dimensional, missing values are pervasive, measurements are irregular, data distributions shift rapidly over time, deployed models require careful maintenance over time. When considered together, learning from time series for health is a uniquely challenging and important area; Significant advancements are required to realize the societal benefits of ML systems for clinical time series. We hope to investigate myriad questions during the workshop.
Machine learning for medical time series poses a set of unique challenges, both in building models of complex, high-dimensional data, and in considering the needs for sensitive, embodied data. While time series are at the heart of many healthcare tasks, there have been few events bringing together researchers in this unique field to assess the state of the field, broadcast promising solutions to hard problems, and outline the main challenges we foresee on the horizon. A core goal of this workshop is to facilitate cutting-edge discussions about work in progress on modeling medical time series and the range of challenges that come with solving this crucial challenge. Through networking events, an exciting line-up of heavy-hitting speakers, and poster sessions with lightning talks, this workshop will shine a new spotlight on an interesting and crucial frontier of machine learning research.
Further, we seek to facilitate new connections and collaborations between researchers to accelerate advancement in our area. To facilitate discussions, there will be breaks with coffee and posters, lightning spotlights to help attendees find similar interests, and a panel discussing research and deployment challenges of time series models in healthcare. Through talks by diverse and heavy-hitting clinical time series researchers—Yan Liu (professor at USC), Emily Fox (professor at Stanford), Stephanie Hyland (senior researcher at MSR), and Danielle Belgrave (senior research scientist at DeepMind))—attendees will broaden their perspectives and learn about state-of-the-art problems and solutions. Like the highly-successful time series workshops at other conferences like KDD and ICML, we expect that ours will be engaging and well-attended.
